Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a world-famous Italian dish that has found its way into kitchens across the globe due to its rich, hearty flavor. Lasagna consists of layers of pasta, cheese, sauce, and either meat or vegetables. It is a baked dish that brings together the warmth and comfort of Italian cuisine in one delicious meal.

Ingredients

These ingredients are the foundation for a traditional lasagna. Feel free to add your own twist to make it unique!

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Grounf beef
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce
  • Tomato paste
  • Olive oil
  • Cheese (as preferred)
  • Salt and pepper (as preferred)
  • Fresh Basil
  • Italian seasoning
  • Egg
  • Spinach

Steps

These steps are my favorite way to prepare lasagna—easy, flavorful, and satisfying!

  1. Prepare the Meat sauce:
    • In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat.
    • Add chopped onions and minced garlic; sauté until softened.
    • Add the ground beef (or pork), cooking until browned. Drain any excess fat.
    • Stir in tomato sauce, tomato paste, salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning. Let the sauce simmer for 20-30 minutes to deepen the flavors.
    • Add fresh basil for extra flavor.
  2. Cook the Lasagna Noodles:
    • Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until they are al dente.
    • Drain and lay the noodles flat on a sheet of parchment paper or a greased surface to avoid sticking.
  3. Prepare the Cheese Mixture:
    • In a large bowl, combine ricotta cheese, an egg, spinach (if using), and a pinch of salt and pepper.
    • Stir in half of the mozzarella and Parmesan cheese to create a rich, creamy mixture.
  4. Layer the Lasagna:
    • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
    • In a large baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce at the bottom.
    • Add a layer of cooked lasagna noodles on top, followed by a layer of the cheese mixture, and then another layer of meat sauce.
    • Repeat this process until you have three or four layers.
    • Finish with a layer of noodles, topped with the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  5. Bake the Lasagna:
    • Cover the lasagna with aluminum foil (to prevent excessive browning) and bake in the preheated oven for 25 minutes.
    • Remove the foil and bake for an additional 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden on top.
  6. Let it Rest and Serve:
    • Remove the lasagna from the oven and let it rest for 10-15 minutes before slicing. This helps the layers set and makes it easier to serve.
    • Garnish with extra basil or Parmesan cheese, and serve with garlic bread on the side for a complete meal.

Enjoy your mouth-watering Lasagna!
